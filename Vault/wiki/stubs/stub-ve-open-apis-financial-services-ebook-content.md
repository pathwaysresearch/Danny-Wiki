## Abstract

This is the Red Hat Special Edition of "Open APIs in Financial Services For Dummies" (Wiley), authored by Alessandro Petroni, Alfonso Navío Francés, and Eric Marts. The book explains what APIs are, why open APIs matter for the financial services industry, and how organizations can design, create, and manage open API strategies to meet changing business needs. It positions open APIs as critical enablers of new business opportunities, developer communities, and competitive differentiation for forward-thinking financial services companies.

## Key Claims

- APIs are sets of requirements governing how one software application can communicate and interact with another, but their true value lies in enabling new customer acquisition, business generation, and greater profit
- Open APIs are publicly available APIs that give developers programmatic access to proprietary software applications or web services, and their openness is what is "really changing the world"
- Open APIs help more people contribute to success and help forward-thinking companies stand out from the competition
- APIs serve as "front doors" that automatically open to let new customers in and are at the center of developer communities aiming to collectively change the world
- Organizations need a deliberate open API strategy designed for changing needs, covering creation and management of open APIs

## Topics Covered

API fundamentals and history, open APIs vs. proprietary APIs, API strategy design for financial services, creating and managing open APIs, developer communities and ecosystems, enterprise open source software (Red Hat), hybrid cloud and container technologies, business value of APIs in financial services, API-driven digital transformation, risk and opportunity in API adoption
