# Data Analytics Made Accessible Content

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This is a textbook by Dr. Anil K. Maheshwari (2015) designed to make business intelligence and data mining concepts accessible, particularly for MBA-level students. The book covers the full data processing chain—from raw data through databases, data warehouses, data mining, and data visualization—with practical caselets drawn from domains like sports (Moneyball), education (Khan Academy), and entertainment (Netflix). It is organized into multiple sections spanning BI concepts, core data mining techniques (clustering, association rule mining), text mining, and applied business analytics including dashboards, KPIs, and balanced scorecards.

## Key Claims

- Data from business is mined to generate intelligence, which in turn makes business better in an unending cycle
- Pattern recognition helps cut through complexity and reveal simpler understandable trends, ranging from hard scientific rules to generalizations like the Pareto principle (80/20 rule)
- Executives determine tracking needs based on key performance indexes (KPIs) or key result areas (KRAs), delivered through customized reports and dashboards
- Organizations need to monitor industry, competitors, suppliers, and customers while developing balanced scorecards for internal health
- The K-Means algorithm is presented as a core clustering technique with discussion of selecting the number of clusters and its advantages and disadvantages

## Topics Covered

Business intelligence concepts and applications, data processing chain (data → database → data warehouse → data mining → data visualization), pattern recognition, clustering and K-Means algorithm, association rule mining and Apriori algorithm, text mining and term document matrices, dashboards and KPIs, balanced scorecards, decision-making frameworks, real-world caselets (Moneyball, Khan Academy, Netflix, WhatsApp), data visualization
